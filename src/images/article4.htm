    Winners of Blended Learning Initiative Announced

    April 13, 2016   |   Digital Learning, Raise Your Hand Texas
    AddThis Sharing Buttons
    Share to FacebookShare to TwitterShare to Google+Share to LinkedInShare to PinterestShare to Email

    After nearly a year of planning, workshopping, and healthy competing, four Texas school districts and one public charter school have been chosen as demonstration sites for the Raise Your Hand Texas Raising Blended Learners initiative. They are:

    1.      Birdville ISD (Tarrant County, northeast of Fort Worth)
    2.      Cisco ISD (West Texas, east of Abilene)
    3.      KIPP Houston (Harris County, Houston)
    4.      Pasadena ISD (Harris County, southeast of Houston)
    5.      Point Isabel ISD (Cameron County, Port Isabel)

    These five are a mix of urban, rural, small, and large public school entities. Each of the sites will receive up to $500,000 in grant funding and comprehensive support over three years to help them implement their blended learning concepts.

    Fifteen additional sites were selected for a pilot network, each of which will receive implementation support (without grant funding) to help promote the expansion of blended learning statewide.

    Birdville ISD

    Birdville ISD is located in Tarrant County, northeast of Fort Worth with a population of 25,000 students. To address low literacy and postsecondary readiness, they will implement station rotation and flipped classroom models at three traditional high schools and redesign their alternative high school to a flex model to allow learners to speed up or slow down credit accumulation based on individual needs.

    Cisco ISD

    Cisco ISD is a rural school district in West Texas enrolling 900 students from their community of fewer than 4,000. To elevate all students to their own individual higher levels of academic achievement, Cisco ISD will use a station rotation model to fill gaps and all push students to succeed, as measured by improved performance in math and science at their elementary and middle schools.

    KIPP Houston

    KIPP Houston is a public charter network in Houston and enrolls 12,500 students. To change students’ outcomes and improve college graduation rates by focusing on the math pathway, KIPP Houston will use an in-class flipped classroom, alongside a summer flex model, to help more students successfully complete Algebra I in the 8th grade and continue along the advanced math sequence throughout high school.

    Pasadena ISD

    Pasadena ISD, southeast of Houston, is one of the 15 largest school districts in Texas with approximately 55,400 students. To address low college enrollment and completion, as well as declining STAAR reading scores, Pasadena ISD will use a flex model to increase STAAR reading scores in grades 5-12; increase participants’ scores on targeted postsecondary readiness indicators; and ultimately increase the number of Pasadena ISD graduates who complete college within six years of high school graduation.

    Point Isabel ISD

    Point Isabel ISD is located at the southernmost tip of Texas and enrolls 2,600 students. Responding to the need to provide flexible school schedules for students who work to help support their families, Point Isabel ISD will use a flex model at their high school which will allow personalized learning experiences for all students to improve academic discipline ratings, internship participation, higher education enrollment, and reduce failure rates. Point Isabel ISD will also use station rotation and flipped classroom models in their elementary and middle schools, focusing on creating blended opportunities for all students, particularly English Language Learners and Special Education students.

    “We selected demonstration sites reflective of the overall Texas student population and varying size and diverse needs of our school districts, as well as their capacity to implement a targeted, scalable blended learning program,” said Alison Badgett, interim executive director for Raise Your Hand Texas. “Our goal is to use what we learn to help school leaders across the state personalize instruction and improve student achievement.”

    Read the Full Statewide Press Release

    We’ll be following the progress of the winning teams in the months to come. Visit often for news alerts, updates, and immersive web stories about how the Raising Blended Learners initiative is helping power student-centered learning in Texas.
